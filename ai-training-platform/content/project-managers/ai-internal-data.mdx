## Security First (This is Critical!)

Before we dive into analysis, let's talk about security. This is **the most important part** of this module.

### Rule #1: Anonymise Personal Information

**What is PII?** Personally Identifiable Information:
- Names
- Email addresses
- Phone numbers
- Addresses
- Any information that identifies a person

**What to do:**
- Remove PII before uploading to public AI tools
- Replace names with "User 1", "User 2", etc.
- Remove email addresses and phone numbers
- Only use enterprise-grade tools (like TBS Enterprise ChatGPT) for sensitive data

**Why it matters:**
- Privacy laws (GDPR, Australian Privacy Act)
- Client confidentiality
- Ethical responsibility
- Legal compliance

![Security First](https://media.giphy.com/media/xT9IgzoKnwFNmISR8I/giphy.gif)
*'Security: Always first. Privacy: Always important. AI: I can help, but only with safe data. You: Got it!'*

### Rule #2: Check Client NDAs

**Before uploading client data:**
1. Read the client's NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)
2. Does it allow third-party processing?
3. Is the AI tool you're using approved?
4. When in doubt, ask your legal team or manager

**Safe approach:**
- Use enterprise AI tools (approved by your company)
- Anonymise data before uploading
- Get approval for sensitive projects

### Rule #3: Opt-Out of Training

**Important:** Make sure your data isn't being used to train public AI models.

**How to check:**
- Look for 'Training Data' or 'Model Training' settings
- Turn off data training/sharing
- Use enterprise tools that don't train on your data

**Why it matters:**
- Your data could appear in responses to other users
- Client confidentiality could be breached
- Competitive information could leak

![Privacy Settings](https://media.giphy.com/media/xT9IgzoKnwFNmISR8I/giphy.gif)
*'AI Tool: Use your data to train our model? You: NO. Also You: *turns off training* Privacy: Protected!'*

## Technique 1: The "Interrogator" Method (Analysing Documents)

This technique lets you ask questions about large documents.

### When to Use This

- Annual reports
- Long strategy documents
- Client briefs
- Research papers
- Any large PDF or document

### How It Works

**Step 1:** Upload the document to ChatGPT or Claude

**Step 2:** Ask specific questions:

> "What are the top 3 risks mentioned in the CEO's letter?"

> "Compare the financial outlook of 2024 vs 2023."

> "List all mentions of 'AI' or 'Automation' and summarise the context."

**Step 3:** Get instant answers without reading the whole document!

### Example: Analysing an Annual Report

**The document:** 50-page annual report

**Your questions:**
1. "What are the main challenges the company faces?"
2. "What are their growth strategies?"
3. "How does this year compare to last year?"

**Time saved:** Reading 50 pages = 2 hours. AI analysis = 5 minutes!

## Technique 2: The "Data Cleaner" Method (Fixing Messy Data)

Real-world data is messy. AI can clean it up!

### The Problem with Real Data

**Common issues:**
- Dates in different formats (DD/MM/YYYY, MM-DD-YYYY, etc.)
- Missing information
- Misaligned columns
- Inconsistent formatting
- Duplicate entries

**Manual cleaning:** Hours of tedious work

**AI cleaning:** Minutes of work!

### How to Clean Data with AI

**Step 1:** Upload your messy CSV

**Step 2:** Ask AI:
> "This dataset is messy. Please:
> - Standardise the date format to YYYY-MM-DD
> - Remove any rows with missing email addresses
> - Fix misaligned columns
> - Remove duplicate entries
> - Output the clean version as a CSV code block."

**Step 3:** Copy the cleaned data and use it!

### Example: Cleaning Customer Data

**Messy data:**
```
Name,Email,Date
John,john@email,15/01/2024
Jane,,2024-02-20
Bob,bob@email,20-03-2024
John,john@email,15/01/2024  (duplicate)
```

**AI cleans it:**
```
Name,Email,Date
John,john@email,2024-01-15
Bob,bob@email,2024-03-20
```

**Clean, standardised, ready to use!**

## Common Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

### Mistake #1: Uploading Sensitive Data

**The problem:** Uploading client data or PII to public AI tools

**The solution:** Always anonymise first. Use enterprise tools for sensitive data.

### Mistake #2: Trusting AI Analysis Blindly

**The problem:** Assuming AI found real patterns

**The solution:** Always verify. Check the data yourself. AI can 'hallucinate' patterns.

### Mistake #3: Not Understanding the Data

**The problem:** Asking AI to analyse data you don't understand

**The solution:** Understand your data first. Then ask AI to help you analyse it.

### Mistake #4: Ignoring Privacy Settings

**The problem:** Data being used to train public models

**The solution:** Always check and disable training data options.
